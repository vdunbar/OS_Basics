Open Science: An Introduction for Biology

Open Science: An Introduction for Biology

Sharon Hanna, Jason Pither & Mathew Vis-Dunbar

2021-05-31

Welcome

This text is part of a series.

This first book is an introduction to Open Science; concepts, theories, and applications.

The other two works in this series are:

  • A collection of lab manuals used by Biology at UBCO, with each chapter dedicated to a course; and
  • A guide on best practices for working with biological data in a computational environment, including a glossary of terms used throughout these texts.

These three texts are linked through common concepts, providing discrete connections between theory, application, and practices of Open Science as they are encountered in undergraduate Biology program at UBC Okanagan.

These texts are also complimented by an online portal that allows you visualize these connections between courses in the undergraduate Biology program, highlighting discrete connections between concepts as they are revisited across courses. This portal is availabel here.
